Learning Experiences

I have been given the opportunity to do many different personal development challenges in both my highschool career as well as my limited university life. Things such as my highschool leadership opportunities as well as personal experiences that have taught me priceless life lessons.


---------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------

The first experience I would like to highlight in my Learning Showcase is when I was given the opportunity to compete in a Model UN competition in 2021. This experience was offered to me by my high school best friend, where we were interested in signing up and competing in the competition. We formed a partnership and entered the competition that was taking place on Zoom due to COVID. The task of Model UN involves being given a country that we must model as representative during an official UN meeting. We made ammendments to clauses and pushed the agenda of our designated country. There were a lot of good participants and a lot of debating and model politics. This allowed me to gain first hand experience in the role politics played in the big picture of the world. At the end of the competition, we placed a podium finish which meant we could progress to the final Australia-wide competition. This was a fantastic experience for me as I was able to learn all about the official procedures of politics as well as gain first hand experience on what working for a company and having meetings will be like.

---------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------

The second experiences I would like to highlight is the learning I completed during highschool. I took a specialised course all the way through highschool called Digital Solutions. This subject involved working with computers in a modelled role of Software Developer. This is where I first fell in love with the idea of going into Computer Engineering, as we completed a lot of very specialised course work. Not only were the assessment tasks for this subject engaging and challenging, but they modelled very accurately what getting a job in Software Development would be like. I learnt a lot of important skills like photoshop, audio editing, game development, python and website development. All of these skills have provided to be extremely helpful in the current coursework that I am completing. It taught me patience, problem solving skills and most importantly how to do the things that I knew I would need to know as a Software Engineer.














---------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------

The final experience I would like to reflect on is my first semester of Bachelor of Computer Science at Griffith University. This course has been a true eye opener in terms of what I am going to need to do in the next 3 years in order to have a successful career. This was especially true in terms of the course "Professional Practices in IT". This course has allowed me to truly see what working for a software development company. This is through the weekly workshop tasks that the course co-ordinator organises. This course work has been proven to be helpful for me to practice completing tasks in a group setting. This was done to emulate the idea that I would not be the only one working on a project at the same time, and teaches me collaboration skills as well as compromising. Other courses that I am taking this semester include a python coding course, to more fully understand the language and practice my proficiency. As well as a Computer Systems And Cyber Security course that will teach me how computer systems interact as well as data security which is becoming a more and more important field everyday. These courses are still teaching me plenty each and every week as will the rest of the Bachelor of Computer Science Degree I still have remaining.
